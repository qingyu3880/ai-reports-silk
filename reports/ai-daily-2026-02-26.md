## 3 Key Insights

### Insight 1: The Shift from "Vibe Coding" to "Agentic Engineering" Represents a Paradigm Change
The terminology shift isn't just semantic—it's a recognition that AI coding has evolved from assisted typing to autonomous task execution. Karpathy's declaration that "coding agents basically didn't work before December and basically work since" marks December 2025 as a watershed moment. The implications are profound: developers are transitioning from "writers of code" to "orchestrators of agents." This requires new skills—task decomposition, agent supervision, and quality judgment—rather than syntax memorization.

### Insight 2: Claude Code's $1B Revenue Proves Enterprise AI Agent Demand is Real
Anthropic's financial milestone validates what many suspected: enterprises are willing to pay premium prices for capable AI agents. The research data is equally telling—experienced users auto-approve 40% of sessions, and the 99.9th percentile task duration has doubled to 45+ minutes. This suggests Claude Code is handling increasingly complex, long-running tasks. The "deployment overhang" (models capable of more autonomy than currently granted) indicates significant headroom for growth as trust builds.

### Insight 3: Multi-Agent Systems and Long-Running Tasks Are the Next Frontier
The Perplexity Computer launch and Anthropic's Agent Skills repository point to a clear trend: single-agent systems are giving way to coordinated multi-agent architectures. The ability to run tasks for "hours or even months" represents a fundamental shift from the chatbot paradigm. This enables new use cases—continuous monitoring, background research, and complex multi-step workflows—that were previously impossible. The infrastructure layer (MCP, Agent Skills) is maturing rapidly, suggesting 2026 will be the year of agent orchestration.
